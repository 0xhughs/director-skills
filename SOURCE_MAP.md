## Overlap detected
- The three AI Cinematography `.docx` guides and `Master Guide V3.md` overlap heavily around the seven-pillar prompt structure, time blocking, front-loading, camera/lens vocabulary, micro-textures, and anti-morphing guardrails.
- Grok materials repeat natural-language prompting, front-loading, one-variable iteration, reference-image consistency, positive restatement instead of unsupported negatives, and aspect/duration/API notes.
- Seedance materials repeat asset-role prompting, duration math, shot blocks, one motion verb per shot, audio layers, and stability constraints.
- Kling materials repeat feature routing, I2V, Elements, start/end frame, extension, negative prompts, and hand/morphing failure mitigation.
- Gemini/Veo/Omni materials repeat natural-language structure, audio-aware prompting, references, extension, and negative constraint limits.
- `ai-storyteller` overlaps with the generated suite's story, continuity, video, and QC layers, but adds stronger short-fiction structure, Fountain workflow, golden-image production, audio/post, rights, and provenance guidance.

## Contradictions and cautions
- Several guides say natural language beats tag lists, while the cinematography guides use strict bracketed/pillar templates. The suite resolves this by using universal briefs first, then choosing prose, slot, or model-specific syntax in `model-adaptation`.
- Negative prompting varies by model. Grok and Le Chat guides favor positive restatement or lack separate negative fields; Kling and some video workflows use negative prompts; Veo guidance recommends limiting negative constraints. The suite never assumes universal negative support.
- Aspect ratio, duration, reference limits, audio support, and parameter surfaces are highly model/surface-specific. The suite marks unknown or verify-current-docs where support is not clear.
- Some model claims are time-sensitive as of May/June 2026 and may become outdated quickly.
- Cost, copyrightability, festival/release, and provenance guidance from `ai-storyteller` is operational risk guidance, not legal or financial advice.

## Excluded or reduced material
- Bypass-oriented and explicit-content operational details from `Grok NSFW Rules.md` and filter-bypass notes in Seedance guides were excluded. Only safety, policy volatility, and refusal/rejection cautions were retained.
- Long prompt libraries and examples were not copied. They were generalized into templates, checklists, and example workflows.
- API code snippets were not copied into the suite because the target is broad agent-skills-compatible prompting, not one vendor SDK.

## Missing information
- The combined source set is now stronger on short-fiction structure, AI writing workflow, Fountain screenplay drafting, and release QC. It remains lighter on detailed production scheduling, jurisdiction-specific legal clearance, festival rules, accessibility/localization standards, and executable validation.
- Several mentioned models such as Runway, Pika, Luma, Sora, Hailuo, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, Ideogram, and DALL-E are not deeply profiled in the source guides; the matrix marks many capabilities unknown.
