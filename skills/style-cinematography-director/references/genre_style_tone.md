# Genre, Style, and Tone

Translate genre into repeatable execution rules.

| Genre | Camera | Light/color | Texture/design | Common risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Drama | restrained, close human distance | motivated practicals, soft contrast | lived-in rooms, personal props | melodrama without action |
| Thriller | controlled moves, surveillance angles | hard shadows, cool contrast | glass, metal, rain, screens | too many reveals in one shot |
| Horror | slow push, locked-off dread, negative space | pockets of light, darkness with detail | decay, dampness, stale air | jump scares without setup |
| Sci-fi | clean geometry or industrial realism | cold practicals, emissive panels | interfaces, worn tech, scale cues | generic blue glow |
| Noir | low angles, frames within frames | hard side light, silhouettes | smoke, blinds, wet streets | unreadable darkness |
| Comedy | wider frames, readable blocking | bright or flat visibility | ordinary spaces, contrast between action and setting | over-stylizing |
| Documentary | handheld or observational | available light | imperfect locations, real-world mess | looking too staged |
| Romance | close distance, gentle motion | warm practicals or soft natural light | tactile details, personal objects | sentiment without specificity |
| Commercial | clean hero framing | controlled highlights | product surfaces, brand palette | busy props distracting from product |

Use references as inspiration. Do not ask for an exact protected scene, logo, character, or living artist imitation when a descriptive visual style works.
