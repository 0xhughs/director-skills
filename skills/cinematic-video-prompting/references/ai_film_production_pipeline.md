# AI Film Production Pipeline

Use this when video prompts are part of a finished short-film workflow rather than isolated clips.

## Practical sequence

1. Concept and script breakdown: logline, treatment, beat sheet, screenplay, shot list.
2. Visual lock: golden images for characters, locations, props, costumes, and palette.
3. Storyboard: verify composition and sequence before spending generations.
4. Batch generation: create 2-3 variations per shot, favor I2V when continuity matters, keep shots short.
5. Audio generation: dialogue, ambience, foley, and music or temp score.
6. Assembly: cut clips together; trim weak AI reaction time and avoid lingering on artificial micro-expression gaps.
7. Post polish: uniform color grade, consistent grain/texture, subtitles/captions, upscale if needed.
8. Release assets: posters, vertical trailer, thumbnails, social clips.

## Prompt implications

- Every video prompt should include an editing note: where it starts, where it ends, and how it cuts.
- Long scenes should be broken into controllable shots.
- Keep audio beds active; silence exposes synthetic footage.
- Track rights/provenance for voices, music, reference images, and AI outputs when release is intended.
