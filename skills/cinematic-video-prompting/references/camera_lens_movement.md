# Camera, Lens, and Movement

## Lens language
- 18-24mm: wide, immersive, spatial distortion, kinetic proximity.
- 28-35mm: naturalistic documentary presence, environmental context.
- 50mm: balanced human perspective, restrained drama.
- 75-100mm: portrait compression, isolation, emotional scrutiny.
- Macro: tactile detail, evidence, fragility, product texture.
- Anamorphic: wide cinematic frame, oval bokeh, horizontal flares; use only when the wide format supports the scene.

## Camera movement
Use one dominant move per short video prompt.
- Locked-off: tension, deadpan comedy, surveillance, realism.
- Slow dolly-in: attention, realization, dread, intimacy.
- Pull-back: reveal, loss, loneliness, scale.
- Tracking: pursuit, routine, journey, kinetic momentum.
- Handheld: immediacy, documentary energy, instability.
- Crane/rise: revelation, scale, transcendence.
- Orbit: subject examination, glamour, uncertainty; risky for identity in weaker models.
- Pan/tilt: discovery, geography, causal reveal.

## Movement guardrails
- Pair camera motion with subject motion only when they do not compete.
- For AI video, prefer simple verbs: walks, turns, reaches, kneels, lifts, exhales.
- State physical consequence: coat swings, glass trembles, dust lifts, water ripples.
- Avoid mirrors, complex hand interactions, crowds, and fast rotations unless essential.
