# Lighting and Photorealism

Photorealism improves when prompts describe physical light and material response instead of generic quality words.

## Lighting controls
- Direction: camera-left, camera-right, overhead, backlight, underlight, window-side.
- Quality: soft, hard, diffused, specular, bounced, practical, fluorescent, sodium vapor, neon.
- Source: window, candle, monitor, streetlight, moon, overcast sky, softbox, bare bulb.
- Contrast: high-key, low-key, chiaroscuro, silhouette, rim light, fill ratio.
- Color temperature: cool blue dawn, warm tungsten, sickly green fluorescent, mixed neon.

## Realism anchors
- Skin: pores, fine lines, natural asymmetry, subsurface warmth, no waxy smoothing.
- Fabric: weave, wrinkles, seams, damp edges, lint, worn cuffs.
- Environment: dust, condensation, scratches, fingerprints, water streaks, chipped paint.
- Optics: shallow depth of field, halation, soft highlight bloom, film grain, slight lens breathing.

## Anti-plastic guardrails
Use concise constraints: natural skin texture, imperfect fabric, physical shadows, grounded contact, realistic reflections, no waxy CGI surface.
