# Story Development Workflow

## Idea intake
Capture: premise seed, protagonist, desire, obstacle, stakes, genre, tone, runtime, production limits, ending instinct, and visual promise.

## Concept expansion
1. Define the promise: what should the audience feel by the end?
2. Define the pressure: what makes action necessary now?
3. Define the contradiction: what makes the protagonist's goal emotionally or morally difficult?
4. Define the cinematic engine: what repeated action, location, ritual, object, or countdown can carry the short?
5. Define the turn: what discovery or decision changes the meaning of the opening?

## Short-film shapes
- Single-location confrontation.
- Countdown or deadline.
- Ritual interrupted.
- Object-driven mystery.
- Silent visual transformation.
- Circular ending with changed meaning.
- Before/after portrait of a choice.

## Development output
A strong concept package includes logline, premise, theme, character engine, conflict, visual tone, scene list, ending, and revision risks.
