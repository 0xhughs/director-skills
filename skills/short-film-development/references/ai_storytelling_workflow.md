# AI Storytelling Workflow

Use AI as a structured collaborator, not as an unattended author. The useful pattern is: generate options, interrogate them, choose with human taste, then revise deliberately.

## Short-story foundations

A compressed story needs intensity, not sprawl. Before expanding an idea, answer:
- Who is involved?
- What happened or is happening?
- When and where does it happen?
- Why does it matter now?
- What larger point, feeling, or question does it carry?

## Nine-element story check

| Element | Practical test |
|---|---|
| Premise | Can the central situation be stated in one or two sentences? |
| Character | What does the protagonist want right now? |
| Conflict | What blocks that want internally or externally? |
| Setting | Why must the story happen here and now? |
| Plot | Does each scene change the situation? |
| Theme | What larger idea is being tested through action? |
| Tone | What attitude should the reader/viewer feel? |
| Point of view | Who is the best lens for limited information? |
| Ending | What changed, and why does the last image/line matter? |

## AI-assisted process

1. Plan: generate premise angles, logline variants, structural options, and risk notes.
2. Interrogate: ask one question at a time about desire, fear, contradiction, setting pressure, and ending.
3. Draft modularly: scene by scene, never the whole story or film at once.
4. Voice-prime carefully: if the user provides style samples, use them to match pacing and vocabulary; do not invent an authorial voice from stereotypes.
5. Revise by pass: structure, character logic, scene tension, dialogue/subtext, imagery, ending payoff, and prose compression.
6. Package: convert the chosen story into logline, synopsis, treatment, beat sheet, screenplay, shot list, and prompt package.

## Structural options

- Unity of effect: every choice builds toward one emotional impact.
- Fichtean crisis curve: begin in pressure, escalate through crises, climax, then resolve quickly.
- Before/after arc: establish state, disrupt it, force a turn, reveal changed state.
- Keyhole focus: restrict the field of view; leave out everything that does not serve the central crisis.

## AI-voice risks

Watch for generic moral lessons, over-explained feelings, cliche phrases, smooth but interchangeable dialogue, and endings that announce the theme instead of dramatizing consequence.
