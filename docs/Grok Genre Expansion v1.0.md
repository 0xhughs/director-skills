# Grok Genre Expansion v1.0

> **How to use this file:** This is the deep-reference companion to the Master Guide. Nothing has been compressed or dropped. Every genre entry is fully documented here, with sub-genre breakdowns, worked example prompts, Aurora-specific failure notes, and workarounds. Where both guides cover the same genre, both versions are preserved and cross-referenced.

---

## Architecture of Each Genre Entry

Each entry includes:
1. **Visual Grammar** — Lighting, camera, lens, palette/grade
2. **DP/Director References** — Confirmed Aurora-parsed names
3. **Aurora-Specific Notes** — Failure modes and workarounds
4. **Worked Example Prompt(s)** — Ready-to-paste, 80–130 words
5. **Starter Prefix** — One-line genre anchor for quick deployment

## SECTION 1 — ACTION, THRILLER, WAR

---

### 1.1 Action / Action-Thriller

**Visual Grammar:**
- Lighting: Hard key light, anamorphic flares, practical sodium streetlamps, muzzle-flash bloom, hot rim light, high-contrast chiaroscuro
- Camera: Whip pans, crash zooms, low-angle hero shots, tracking follows, speed-ramps to slow-mo, crane reveals, Dutch tilts
- Lens: Anamorphic 1.8x at 2.39:1, wide 21mm for chases, 35mm hero, 85mm reactions
- Grade: Teal-and-orange or bleach-bypass, crushed blacks, Kodak 5219

**DP/Director References:** Michael Bay, Nolan/Hoytema, John Wick/Stahelski, Greig Fraser, Mann/Spinotti, Michael Mann electric magenta-cyan cityscapes, Sicario desaturated steel blues, John Wick Snorricam precision

**Mood Adjectives:** Relentless, taut, propulsive, breakneck, visceral, paranoid, lethal, kinetic, sleek, brooding

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Add `real-time speed, no slow motion` for full-speed combat — Aurora defaults to slow-mo
- Use `shot on Phantom Flex 4K` for intentional slow-mo punctuation at impact frames
- One camera move only — crash zoom + tracking shot + whip pan produces morphing artifacts
- Handheld works for combat; use Steadicam for tactical movement

**Worked Example:**
> A black '67 Mustang weaves through Los Angeles freeway traffic at dusk, pursued by two matte-black SUVs. Low-angle tracking shot from bumper height, anamorphic 35mm, snap-zoom to driver's eyes when she shifts gears. Sodium-orange highlights, crushed shadows, anamorphic lens flares, teal-and-orange grade, bleach-bypass film grain, Michael Mann Heat aesthetic. Speed-ramp from 24fps to 60fps slow-mo on the gear shift — sparks fly as the Mustang sideswipes a guardrail. Real-time speed, no slow motion on approach. AUDIO: roaring V8, percussive sub-bass score, screeching rubber, distant freeway hum.

**Starter Prefix:** *Cinematic action thriller, anamorphic 2.39:1, teal-and-orange grade, low-angle handheld tracking shot, IMAX-scale, in the style of Michael Mann and Hoyte van Hoytema.*

### 1.2 War / Military / Historical Epic

**Visual Grammar:**
- Lighting: Volumetric god rays through smoke, low-key high-contrast, blown-out overcast battlefield sky, cool desaturated ash, firelight muzzle-flash strobing
- Camera: Sweeping aerial over battlefield, crane up over trenches, low-angle tracking with running soldier, shoulder-mounted handheld, slow-mo dolly over fallen (Malick)
- Lens: 35mm anamorphic for epic, 70mm IMAX for awe, 16–24mm wide handheld for visceral combat, 50mm Spielberg combat lens
- Grade: Bleach-bypass desaturated (Saving Private Ryan), teal-and-orange (Fury Road), sepia period, Kodak Vision3 500T heavy grain, cold blue-grey northern

**DP/Director References:** Spielberg/Kamiński, Nolan/Hoytema, Mendes/Deakins (1917), Malick, Kubrick, Kurosawa, Mel Gibson, Ridley Scott, Peter Jackson

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Crowds degrade past 6 visible humans — render hero foreground, mass becomes distant army of silhouettes in fog
- Use `shoulder-mounted handheld, lens spattered with seawater` for visceral combat
- `Bleach-bypass desaturated war grade` is a confirmed Aurora-parsed phrase

**Worked Example:**
> A 22-year-old American soldier crouches behind a steel hedgehog as machine-gun rounds spark off it — he glances up at the sky, jaw clenched. Shoulder-mounted handheld, 24mm wide, lens spattered with seawater, slight focus hunt. Bleach-bypass desaturated war grade, blown-out overcast sky, hard side-light, heavy 16mm film grain, smoke haze. Real-time speed, no slow motion — occasional shake on nearby impacts. AUDIO: muted sub-bass thuds, ringing tinnitus, distant orders, no music.

**Alternative Approach (Apocalypse Now palette):**
War/military is Janusz Kamiński's bleach-bypass standard — drained color, retained silver, sickly greens and concrete greys, 90° handheld with a 45° shutter for staccato motion, blood and sand smearing the lens, 35mm with stripped lens coatings, and either ground-level extreme low POV (Saving Private Ryan) or oner long-take Steadicam (1917). Apocalypse Now extends the palette into lurid orange napalm.

### 1.3 Crime / Heist / Gangster

**Visual Grammar:**
- Lighting: Low-key hard side-light, venetian-blind shadow stripes, motivated practicals, smoke-filled backroom with green banker's lamp, car headlights as key
- Camera: Slow push-in on watching eyes (Goodfellas/Sopranos signature), Steadicam Copa-shot oner, locked-down surveillance, low-angle dolly under poker table
- Lens: Panavision anamorphic 35mm (Mann), 40–50mm Scorsese intimacy, 27mm Arri Master Anamorphic for heist tension, shallow 85mm interrogation
- Grade: Teal-orange (Mann night), green-tinted (Fincher), warm tungsten (Scorsese restaurant), piss-yellow (Chinatown)

**DP/Director References:** Scorsese/Ballhaus, Mann/Beebe/Spinotti, Coppola/Willis, Fincher/Cronenweth, Melville, Refn/Drive

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- The Goodfellas slow push-in is a reliable Aurora anchor — use `slow push-in on watching eyes, Scorsese aesthetic`
- Use `motivated practicals, banker's lamp pool` for interior crime atmosphere
- Avoid multi-character poker scenes — Aurora loses track of hand positions

**Worked Example (Heist):**
> A masked thief crouches over a glowing red-LED safe panel, types six digits, breath visible — the lock thunks open. Tight 50mm over-the-shoulder, then slow push-in on the LED display. Low-key, single overhead practical, hard shadows, teal-orange Michael Mann grade. Real-time, fingers move precisely, single bead of sweat slides at beat 6. AUDIO: low pulsing synth bass, faint clicks of internal mechanism, glove-leather creak, no music swell until thunk.

**Gangster Variant:**
> A silver-haired don in a chalk-stripe suit holds court at the back booth of an empty restaurant — two capos sit across, eyes down. Steadicam slow push-in from the restaurant entrance, 35mm Panavision anamorphic, Scorsese/Ballhaus aesthetic. Warm tungsten restaurant key, deep shadows in the booth, smoke curling from an ashtray. His hands stay still on the table — only his eyes move. AUDIO: distant kitchen, faint Sinatra from the bar, low conversation murmur, no score.

## SECTION 2 — HORROR

---

### 2.1 Psychological Horror

**Visual Grammar:**
- Lighting: Unsettling symmetry, sterile interiors, color-blocking (sickly green, bone-white, blood crimson accent), soft natural light with long lenses to flatten space, locked-off slow zoom-ins toward doors and faces
- Camera: Locked-off symmetrical wide, handheld breathing camera, slow dolly-in on eyes
- Lens: Wide 18–24mm for distortion, 50mm for unease
- Grade: Desaturated bruise-purple, cool institutional grey

**References:** Kubrick/The Shining, Aster/Hereditary, Lynch/Mulholland Drive, Eggers/Blaschke

**Worked Example:**
> A woman sits motionless at a kitchen table at 3 AM, a single hanging bulb swinging slowly above her. Locked-off symmetrical wide on a 35mm lens, then 6-second slow dolly-in to extreme close-up on her unblinking left eye. Robert Eggers Jarin Blaschke aesthetic, candlelight bulb practical, deep ink blacks, desaturated bruise-purple grade, 1.66:1, organic film grain. The bulb sways at one cycle per four seconds — her pupil dilates on the final frame. AUDIO: clock tick, low 40Hz drone, refrigerator hum, a single wet whisper "you're already inside."

---

### 2.2 Supernatural Horror

Gothic and candlelit — single-window God-rays, dust motes, religious iconography, desaturated cool greys with amber candle warmth, Eggers-Blaschke natural-flame-only lighting.

**References:** Robert Eggers/Jarin Blaschke (The Witch, The Lighthouse), Hereditary/Aster

**Worked Example:**
> An iron crucifix on a stone chapel wall, candlelight flickering below it — a shadow moves across the wall independent of any figure. Static locked-off 35mm, 6-second hold, then slow 10-second push-in on the shadow. Robert Eggers aesthetic, candlelight-only practical, cool desaturated grey stone with amber warm candle zones, dust motes in light shafts, deep chiaroscuro. No visible character — shadow only. AUDIO: wind through stone, distant bell toll, low sub-bass drone, single candle flutter.

---

### 2.3 Slasher

Autumnal suburban — Halloween orange and black, fog, jack-o-lanterns, 1978 anamorphic, Carpenter-Cundey backlit silhouettes of the killer in deep background negative space.

**References:** Carpenter/Cundey, Argento (Bava blue slasher palette)

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Aurora softens explicit gore — imply via `split skin, dark stain spreading` rather than describe wounds graphically
- Backlit killer silhouette in deep negative space is a reliable Aurora anchor

**Worked Example:**
> A teenage girl walks down a fog-filled suburban street at night, jack-o-lanterns on every porch — a tall silhouetted figure stands motionless under a streetlamp 200 feet behind her. Locked-off 35mm wide, static. Carpenter/Cundey 1978 anamorphic aesthetic, Halloween orange and black, white streetlamp rim on the silhouette, deep shadow negative space, fog diffusing the background. She doesn't look back. The figure hasn't moved. AUDIO: distant wind, dry leaves on asphalt, slow heartbeat pulse, no music.

---

### 2.4 Body Horror

Clinical sterile white-and-teal contrasted with visceral wet textures and surgical lamp pools — Cronenberg's Videodrome, Ducournau's Titane, Fargeat's The Substance — macro 100mm with shallow focus, slow rotating dolly.

**Grade:** Sickly yellow-green flesh (Cronenberg body horror)

**Worked Example:**
> A surgical lamp pools on a chrome operating table — the surface is wrong, too warm, moving slightly. 100mm macro, slow 180° rotating dolly, extreme shallow focus. Clinical sterile white-and-teal grade, surgical lamp hard overhead, wet surfaces catching specular. Cronenberg Videodrome aesthetic. Real-time speed. AUDIO: fluorescent hum, distant ventilation, single wet sound.

---

### 2.5 Found Footage

VHS handicam — chroma noise, time-code burn-in, dropped frames, fisheye 8mm distortion.

**References:** Blair Witch Project/Myrick, Cloverfield/Abrams

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Whispered dialogue often comes out clear — specify `hoarse trembling whisper, low-passed, distant`
- Use `time-code burn-in, chroma noise, tape warble` to get authentic found-footage degradation

**Worked Example:**
> Shaky VHS camcorder footage in a pitch-black forest — only a flashlight beam and the operator's panicked breathing. Handheld fisheye 8mm distortion, dropped frames, time-code 07/14/22 11:04:1999 burn-in, blown highlights when light hits a tree. Blair Witch aesthetic, chroma noise, tape warble, no music. The operator spins 180° — beam catches a pale figure at the edge of frame for 3 frames, then the camera falls. AUDIO: ragged breathing, snapping twigs, wind, then a child's giggle that warps into static.

## SECTION 3 — SCIENCE FICTION

---

### 3.1 Hard Sci-Fi

Sterile, cyan, slate-grey, austere — 2001's HAL-red-on-cyan, Interstellar's panel-light corridors, Arrival's underexposed atmospherics. IMAX 65mm aspect, locked-off wides, contemplative long takes.

**References:** Villeneuve/Fraser, Kubrick/Unsworth

**Worked Example:**
> An astronaut tumbles slowly outside a rotating space station, Earth filling the frame behind her. Floating handheld 35mm, breath-paced single take, Gravity/Lubezki style. Clinical white-and-charcoal grade, harsh sunlight / ink-black void, no atmospheric haze. Realistic zero-G physics, tether snaking behind her. AUDIO: only her breathing inside the helmet, faint radio static crackle, a single warning beep.

---

### 3.2 Space Opera

Dune-scale saturated complementary palettes — desert sand vs. blue lightsaber, twin sunsets, anamorphic 2.39:1, sweeping crane reveals.

**References:** Greig Fraser/Dune, Lucas/Star Wars, Besson/The Fifth Element

**Worked Example:**
> Two moons rise over a rust-red desert planet — a lone figure in sand-colored robes watches from a cliff edge, robes catching wind. Sweeping crane-up reveal, anamorphic 2.39:1, 24mm wide angle. Dune/Greig Fraser aesthetic, warm amber desert light, deep cobalt sky, volumetric god rays through distant storm, IMAX scale. AUDIO: low brass swell, wind across stone, distant machinery hum.

---

### 3.3 Cyberpunk

Rain-slicked neon megacities — cyan-teal night, magenta and orange neon punch, heavy atmospheric haze always, slow majestic Villeneuve-scale wides where humans are dwarfed by brutalist architecture.

**References:** Blade Runner 2049/Roger Deakins, Scott/Cronenweth, Shirow/Oshii, Syd Mead, Moebius

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- The single most powerful cyberpunk anchor: `Blade Runner 2049 aesthetic, Roger Deakins lighting, ARRI Alexa, anamorphic 2.39:1`
- `Wet asphalt neon reflections` is a flagship Aurora strength — lean into it

**Worked Example:**
> A lone hacker in a translucent black raincoat steps out of a ramen stall in Neo-Kowloon at 2 AM — rain falls upward into the neon. Low-angle wide 24mm, slow handheld push-in, then crash-zoom to her augmented eye reflecting Cantonese kanji. Blade Runner 2049 Roger Deakins aesthetic, electric blue hot magenta, volumetric haze, chromatic aberration, wet asphalt reflections, Masamune Shirow detail density. Her coat ripples in updraft from a passing hovercar. AUDIO: synthwave bassline 90bpm, sizzling neon hum, distant rain, holographic ad jingle in Mandarin.

---

### 3.4 Dystopian

Desaturated concrete and sickly amber — clinical white charcoal (hard sci-fi variant), sodium-vapor dystopian streetlamps, CRT phosphor green.

**References:** Scott/Cronenweth, Children of Men/Lubezki

**Worked Example:**
> A long queue of grey-clad workers shuffles through a checkpoint gate under sodium floodlights — a single guard watches from an elevated booth. Wide telephoto 135mm compressed, locked-off static. Desaturated cold grey grade, sodium-vapor amber overhead, clinical white-on-grey signage, heavy grain. Brazil/Gilliam aesthetic. Real-time speed, no slow motion. AUDIO: industrial ventilation, shuffling feet, intercom crackle, no music.

---

### 3.5 Retrofuturism

70s warm orange-mustard-brown or 80s synthwave purple-teal — vintage 16mm for retrofuturism, analog CRT interfaces, chrome bubble shapes.

**References:** 70s: Star Wars ANH/Lucas/Taylor. 80s: Tron, Stranger Things magenta-and-cyan

**Worked Example:**
> A retro-futurist control room lined with blinking amber dials and CRT monitors showing orbital trajectories — a female scientist in a burnt-orange jumpsuit adjusts a lever. Locked-off 35mm medium shot, slow push-in over 6 seconds. 1970s retrofuturism aesthetic, warm orange-mustard-brown tungsten key, cool CRT phosphor green fill, Kodak 5247 emulation, heavy grain. AUDIO: Moog synthesizer drone, computer bleep cascade, ventilation hum, no dialogue.

## SECTION 4 — DRAMA & ROMANCE

---

### 4.1 Literary / Prestige Drama

Naturalistic and restrained — muted earthy palette, bleach-bypass for prestige, available motivated practicals, golden-hour and window-light key, shallow 50mm/85mm DOF, locked-off composed wides, slow imperceptible push-ins.

**References:** PT Anderson/Elswit, Brady Corbet/Crawley, Sidney Lumet, Joe Wright/McGarvey, Kubrick/Alcott, Lubezki, Deakins, Kamiński, Coppola, Reichardt

**Worked Example:**
> An elderly father and his adult daughter sit across a kitchen table in soft morning light — she places her hand near his without touching it. Locked-off symmetrical two-shot on 35mm, slow 10-second push-in to her hand. Lol Crawley/Brady Corbet aesthetic, north-facing window as soft key, muted earth tones, faint Kodak grain, 1.66:1. Steam rises from two cups — her thumb twitches once. AUDIO: mantel clock tick, distant traffic, refrigerator hum, no music — she finally whispers "I read your letter."

---

### 4.2 Family Drama

Kitchen-amber palette — practical lamps as motivated key, Kodak Portra 400.

**Worked Example:**
> Three siblings in their 30s sit around a hospital waiting-room table at 2 AM — one stares at her phone, one has his head in his hands, the third watches a muted TV. Static locked-off 50mm, slightly high angle. Available fluorescent overhead, one warm lamp in corner, Kodak Portra 400 grade, subtle 35mm grain. No movement for 8 seconds. AUDIO: fluorescent hum, distant hospital PA, vending machine hum, no music.

---

### 4.3 Courtroom Drama

Cool fluorescent warm wood — static medium two-shot, OTS reverse.

**References:** Sidney Lumet (12 Angry Men), Joe Wright

**Worked Example:**
> A public defender stands at the defense table, hands gripping the rail, facing a skeptical judge — jury box visible in background. Static two-shot 35mm, eye-level. Cool fluorescent overhead with warm mahogany fill, clean broadcast grade, deep focus showing jury faces. She takes a breath, then begins. AUDIO: courtroom murmur settling to silence, paper shuffle, single pen click, her first word.

---

### 4.4 Period Drama

Vermeer painterly light — Kodak Portra 400 or Barry Lyndon candlelight (Kubrick/Alcott), slow dolly across room.

**References:** Kubrick/Alcott (Barry Lyndon), Joe Wright, Merchant Ivory

**Worked Example:**
> A Victorian lady in a deep burgundy gown reads a letter by candlelight at a writing desk — outside, snow falls against the window. Static Rembrandt portrait, 50mm, then slow 8-second push-in. Barry Lyndon zero-fill candlelight aesthetic, warm amber flame as sole key, soft spill on burgundy silk, deep mahogany shadow, 1.66:1. Candle flame sways once. AUDIO: quill scratch, distant wind, fire crackle, no music.

---

### 4.5 Romance / Romantic Drama

Saturated warm reds, ambers, golds (Wong Kar-wai) or pastel dreaminess (La La Land) or teal twilight melancholy — soft diffused golden-hour, candle and practical warmth, halation around bulbs, Pro-Mist filtration.

**References:** Wong Kar-wai/Christopher Doyle, Linklater, Guadagnino/Mukdeeprom, Gerwig, Celine Song

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Lip-sync warps facial geometry on head-on kisses — frame as profile or three-quarter, or cut just before lips meet

**Worked Example (Viral Rain-Kiss Formula):**
> Two lovers caught in a sudden Tokyo rainstorm pull each other under a neon convenience-store awning. Slow 360° orbit at chest height, 35mm, slight handheld breathing. Wong Kar-wai Christopher Doyle saturated emerald and ruby neon, water droplets caught in rim light, slick wet skin, anamorphic flares. Her hand slides up his soaked collar — rain falls in slow-motion at 60fps. AUDIO: ambient rain on awning, distant scooter hiss, slow Mandopop ballad piano, soft inhale — no spoken line.

**WKW Alley Variant:**
> Slow tracking medium shot of a subject in a saturated red-and-green Hong Kong alley, step-printed slow-motion, neon halation, 35mm Kodak, shallow 85mm DOF, in the style of Wong Kar-wai's In the Mood for Love, Christopher Doyle cinematography, longing and intoxicating.

## SECTION 5 — THRILLER & MYSTERY

---

### 5.1 Psychological Thriller / Crime Thriller

Single hard side-key, venetian-blind shadows, fluorescent buzz, monitor-glow blue, interrogation underlight, server-rack LED.

**References:** Fincher/Cronenweth/Messerschmidt, Mann/Spinotti, Pakula/Willis, Tony Scott/Mindel, Soderbergh

**Worked Example:**
> A detective sits alone in a fluorescent-lit precinct at 2 AM, scrolling a manila file — a single desk lamp burns. Locked-off telephoto 100mm from across the room, slow 8-second push-in. Fincher/Erik Messerschmidt desaturated cyan grade, lifted blacks, hard fluorescent buzz, monitor glow on face, digital RED-camera clarity, 2.39:1. He turns one page, freezes — the lamp flickers once. AUDIO: ballast hum, page slide, distant radio dispatch chatter, low 30Hz drone.

---

### 5.2 Mystery / Detective

Film-noir low-key, venetian-blind slats across face, hard single-source from window, green desk lamp pool, fog-filtered headlights, top-down interrogation overhead.

**References:** Fincher/Cronenweth/Messerschmidt, Polanski, Fukunaga, Villeneuve/Deakins, Bong Joon-ho, Argento (giallo)

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Min Choi tip: each sentence in your prompt = one beat of held time — write fewer, longer sentences for slow-burn mystery

**Worked Example:**
> A crime scene investigator crouches over a chalk outline on wet cobblestone, torch beam picking out a single glove — she doesn't touch it. Crane-down from above, locked-off birds-eye, then slow rack focus to her eyes. Fincher/Cronenweth green-tinted digital noir, top-down interrogation overhead, mist catching the torch beam, charcoal-and-blood-red giallo undertone. AUDIO: distant rain, leather boot on stone, low 30Hz drone, no music.

---

### 5.3 Political Thriller / Techno-Thriller

Pakula muted grey-green, monitor-glow magenta/cyan, server-rack LED environments.

**References:** Pakula/Willis (All the President's Men), Tony Scott/Mindel, Soderbergh

**Worked Example:**
> A intelligence analyst in a dark server room stares at three screens of cascading data — red WARNING banners bloom across one monitor. Static locked-off 75mm, eye-level. Cool blue server LED ambient, monitor-glow magenta-cyan key, deep shadow zones, Fincher/David Fincher digital-noir grade. Her finger hovers over Enter. AUDIO: server fan hum, keyboard click, distant air conditioning, silence as she decides.

## SECTION 6 — COMEDY

---

### 6.1 Slapstick

Wide locked-off symmetrical framing (Keaton, Tati), undercranked fast-motion, high-key flat lighting, 4:3 Academy ratio.

**References:** Buster Keaton, Jacques Tati, Edgar Wright

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Comic timing must be baked in explicitly — `holds the look for 2 seconds in silence, then deadpans...`
- Min Choi's beat trick: write each beat as a separate sentence — Aurora respects sequential sentences as timeline ordering

---

### 6.2 Romantic Comedy

High-key with primary-color pops, saturated cream/butter-yellow, eye-level static medium shots, 35mm shallow DOF.

**References:** Nora Ephron, Richard Curtis, Greta Gerwig

**Worked Example:**
> A woman in a yellow raincoat runs across a rain-soaked London street to catch a red double-decker bus — she misses it, turns, and there he is holding an umbrella. Medium shot, 35mm, eye-level, slight handheld breathing. High-key with primary-color pops, saturated cream-yellow palette, Pro-Mist filtration, warm Richard Curtis aesthetic. She laughs — he doesn't yet know why. AUDIO: light piano, London rain, distant bus engine, her laughter.

---

### 6.3 Dark Comedy

Wes Anderson pastel symmetrical deadpan, or Coen Brothers earthy bleakness, or Lanthimos clinical detachment — flat slightly-deadpan lighting and centered composition.

**References:** Wes Anderson/Robert Yeoman, Coen Brothers, Yorgos Lanthimos

---

### 6.4 Mockumentary

Handheld with awkward push-ins, talking-head locked-off interviews, flat office fluorescents.

**References:** Christopher Guest, Mike Schur (The Office), Ricky Gervais

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Deliberately awkward timing must be written explicitly: `awkward 2-second pause before the sigh`
- `No laugh track` is a required closing phrase for mockumentary

**Worked Example:**
> A 40-something man in a beige cubicle stares directly at the camera after his coworker walks away. He takes a slow sip of stale coffee, mouths "wow," then sighs. Locked-off talking-head interview, then a clumsy 1-second zoom-in on his face as he reacts. Flat office fluorescents, slightly desaturated, slightly green, available light only, documentary feel, 24mm wide. Real-time speed — deliberately awkward 2-second pause before the sigh. AUDIO: AC hum, distant printer, no music, no laugh track.

## SECTION 7 — FANTASY

---

### 7.1 Epic High Fantasy

LOTR scope — sweeping aerial vistas, golden magic-hour, volumetric god-rays through ancient pines, lush emerald-and-gold palette, 35mm grain.

**References:** Jackson/Lesnie, Reeves/Fraser, David Lowery

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Aurora is explicitly strong on fantasy creatures — dragon scales, wings, and fantasy environments are flagship strengths
- Multi-creature battles glitch past 2 figures — render hero vs. single foe; armies become silhouettes in mist

**Worked Example (Dragon):**
> A crimson dragon with iridescent scales bursts through storm clouds above a snow-capped mountain range, wings creating wind vortices. Sweeping aerial drone tracking shot, anamorphic 2.39:1, slow crane-up reveal as it passes. Peter Jackson/Andrew Lesnie, volumetric god rays piercing clouds, golden magic-hour, deep emerald valley below, WETA-grade detail. Scales catch sunlight — single tail-whip slow-motion. AUDIO: mythic horn fanfare, choir, leathery wing flap, low dragon roar, wind howl.

---

### 7.2 Dark Fantasy

Del Toro's amber-and-teal labyrinth — single candle/lantern key, deep chiaroscuro, twisted forests, baroque costuming, Eggers-Blaschke natural-flame technique.

**References:** Del Toro/Navarro (Pan's Labyrinth, Shape of Water), Robert Eggers

**Worked Example:**
> A pale faun with spiral horns crouches at the entrance to a mossy stone labyrinth at night, a lantern casting amber on his bark-like skin. Static 35mm medium shot, then slow push-in over 8 seconds. Del Toro/Guillermo Navarro aesthetic, single lantern as key, deep chiaroscuro shadows, amber-and-teal grade, twisted branch silhouettes. He extends one hand. AUDIO: wind through stone, distant owl, low brass drone, single chime.

---

### 7.3 Urban Fantasy

Modern desaturated city greys with magic accent colors as motivated kicker — electric blue runes, gold sigils (Constantine, John Wick, Doctor Strange).

**Worked Example:**
> A woman in a black coat traces a glowing gold sigil on a subway window as the train moves — the symbols pulse once, then fade. Handheld tracking alongside her, 35mm, slight motion blur from the train. Desaturated urban grey base with gold sigil accent light as sole warm source, cool blue transit fluorescents overhead. Constantine/dark urban fantasy aesthetic. AUDIO: train rhythm, electric hum of sigil activation, ambient transit crowd, no score.

---

### 7.4 Fairy Tale

Pastel storybook — illuminated-manuscript gold, watercolor texture, Ghibli palette (if animated).

**Aurora-Specific Notes:**
- Ghibli requires `watercolor texture, soft brushstrokes` — without it, defaults to generic anime

**Worked Example:**
> A round forest spirit with leaf-like ears sits beneath a glowing mushroom in light rain. Static medium 50mm, soft 6-second handheld breath. Studio Ghibli watercolor texture, Miyazaki palette — pastel emerald and sky-blue, soft brushstrokes, childlike wonder. Rain-drops bead on its fur, eyes blink slowly, one ear twitches. AUDIO: Joe Hisaishi-style piano motif, rain patter, forest ambience, a soft whimsical chime.

## SECTION 8 — DOCUMENTARY

---

### 8.1 Nature Documentary

BBC Planet Earth — Cineflex heli-gimbal aerial telephoto, 600–1200mm wildlife isolation, Phantom 1000fps slow-mo predator strikes, Laowa probe-lens macro, golden-hour worship, richly saturated naturalism.

**References:** BBC NHU/Doug Allan, Werner Herzog, Attenborough narration cadence

**Worked Example:**
> A snow leopard crouches on a Himalayan ridge at dawn, breath visible, watching a Markhor goat 200m below. 600mm telephoto compressed shot from a hide, locked tripod, slow 4-second rack focus from leopard's eye to distant prey. BBC NHU/Doug Allan aesthetic, dawn alpenglow, National Geographic grade — deep blues / warm amber rock, ultra-sharp detail. Tail flicks once — cloud shadow drifts across ridge. AUDIO: wind, distant ungulate bleat, calm Attenborough-cadence narration: "In the quiet of these heights, patience is everything."

---

### 8.2 Observational / Cinéma Vérité

Handheld 16mm fly-on-the-wall, available fluorescent light, no scoring.

**References:** Frederick Wiseman, Pennebaker, Maysles

**Worked Example:**
> Workers on a factory floor assemble circuit boards at long tables under fluorescent lights — one woman pauses, rubs her eyes, then continues. Handheld 16mm wide-angle, observational distance, slight auto-focus hunting. Available fluorescent light, flat ungraded natural color, no motivated lighting, documentary grain. Real-time, no theatrical staging. AUDIO: factory hum, assembly-line clicks, distant foreman, no music.

---

### 8.3 Expository / Talking Head
